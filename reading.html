Freakonomics - The Unorthodox Ways Our World Works

The title’s innocuous enough. Take a glance, and you’d probably come to the conclusion that Freakonomics is a book about economics, and you’re not interested in that right now. But I’m sure you’ve been taught not to judge a book by its cover.

Freakonomics is, at its core, an investigative look into the many assumptions and fallacies that we take for granted. With its cross-disciplinary examination of distinct social phenomenon – the similarities between the mindsets of the Ku Klux Klan and Real Estate Agents, the organizational structures of criminal gangs and McDonalds – each page delivers challenges to pre-conceived conclusions and uncovers the true nature of our complex society.
